Gaming Contest (Fifa) Rules

General Rules :
  1. All competitors are required to stay within the tournament area or inform the organizers in the event of stepping out.
  2. All players must show up at the venue before the tournament and report to the organizer’s control table to collect their ENTRY AND GROUP NUMBERS.
  3. Players not present at the designated start time for any match will be disqualified, and their opponent shall be granted a 3-0 score. Players are encouraged to be in the designated play area 15-minutes prior to game time.
  4. Players will be shown their designated station, set up the game, and immediately begin playing. Each player will have 2 minutes to configure controls, line-ups, and settings according to the rules described above under Match Settings and Tournament Rules. Custom packages cannot be used, nor can anything else that is not available in the in-game pause menu.
  5. Players shall be responsible for keeping track of game situations so that the situation can be restored if a game is interrupted. In all situations possible, tournament referees will monitor the game situation so that it may be restored in the event of game interruptions.
  6. Players shall raise their hands at the end of the game so a referee can record their score. Only scores reported to the referee are official.
  7. In case of any unfortunate event or disconnection, disc error, or electricity failure, the score will remain the same regardless of the game played, but the half in which the problem occurred will be played again.
  8. You cannot pause the game unless it is stopped, i.e., if it’s a throw-in or the ball is in your own keeper’s possession. If you pause the game intentionally, you may be warned or disqualified at the referee’s discretion. A second warning will disqualify you automatically. You have 40 seconds after the pause.
  9. Players will refrain from using vulgar or offensive language.
  10. In cases of fraud and/or cheats, a participant will be disqualified immediately, and disciplinary measures will be taken.
  11. Referees will be on-site to monitor all gameplay. Referees will begin to play and will record scores for each game. A special I.D. tag will identify referees.
  12. In case of any other dispute, the referee/admin’s decision will be final.
    13. N.B. : The tournament rules may be subject to change at short notice on the spot by the committee or referee.
Tournament Rules :
  1. You can choose your preferred controls; Manual, Semi, or Assisted.
  2. The tactical defending mode only.
  3. You must bring your own controllers. No memory cards or saved games are permitted.
  4. You cannot select any fictional or All-Star teams. Any regolar-season teams, as installed with the original game, can be selected. Both players can choose the same team.
  5. Selecting preset formations (4-3-3 or 4-4-2 etc.) are allowed before the match starts.
  6. Custom formations and editing player position within a formation are not allowed.
  7. Selecting tactics are allowed before the match starts.
  8. Once the match has started, players can only pause the game to change strategy, tactics, and/or formation for one last time. If the player’s health is low, you can change the player. But not strategy tactics or formation.
  9. Set pieces are available. Man-marking is allowed.
  10. No forward pass at kick-off. You must back pass the ball.
FIFA RULES:
** The Tournament will be a knockout 1 vs. 1 tournament.
The opponent will be selected by lottery **
Match Settings
  • Stadium: Neutral
  • Game Tactics: Custom tactics allowed
  • Game speed: Normal
  • Formations: No custom formations
  • Injuries: Off
  • Bookings: On
  • Offside: On
  • Referee: Random
  • Home Team: Coin toss will decide.
  • Half Length: 6 minutes
  • Handball: Off
  • Camera Settings: Tele Broadcast
  • Weather: Overcast
  • Time/Score display: On
  • Heads-up Display (HUD): Player Name Bar and Indicator On
  • Radar: 2D
  • Audio: Music off and Commentary on
  • Player Attributes: According to each team
  • Pitch Pattern: Default
  • Pitch Wear: None
    • Extra Time: In the event of a draw at the end of regulation time, players should use the “Classic” mode for overtime; this mode includes 2 (15 minutes) extra time periods and Penalty Kicks if the match is still tied.
NOTE These are the initial common rules. We will announce the detailed rules along with registration links on our Facebook event page soon. Stay tuned!
